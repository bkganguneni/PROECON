REVIT

Revit is a comprehensive workflow and software product that helps a variety of professionals work together, such as designers, construction professionals, architects and builders. The software provides sophisticated ways to create models of real-world structures and buildings. Revit is mainly utilized in building information modeling (BIM).

In BIM, construction professionals can enter the real-world characteristics of objects, and simulate how they interact with one another without actually building them. Similar to computer-aided draft and design (CADD), BIM attaches attributes to the objects, such as weight or complex interactives like the ability of doors to open and close in the simulation

If you are a building or construction professional, or anyone interested in modeling construction, Revit architecture software is a tool that can assist with conceptualizing and understanding the project you are working on.

Some of the tasks that are provided REVIT for modelling or designing for a construction or a building

1. See Your Project in Virtual Reality or 3D

With Revit, you create a basic layout of the project you are building using a quick selection of items from families of virtual objects. These virtual object families assist users with interior design, as they quickly show the user how an object will look inside the space and allow the user to cycle through multiple options to find the best selection.

2. Huge Library of Parametric Building Gizmos

Revit's libraries are called families, and there are many of them. They contain all the information necessary to simulate real-world construction elements in the simulation.

Need a chair? There's a family for that. Need concrete paving? There's a library for that. How about load-bearing walls made of different materials? There's a family for that too. With Revit, there is no need to re-invent the wheel since you have it all at your fingertips.

3. Greater Accuracy

Due to Revit being a 3D software, there's greater accuracy in the documentation and design of structures. In 2D software, users can often overwrite a structure's dimensions, leading to errors in the final documentation and construction. The 3D software won't let users overwrite the numbers, as the 3D models will prevent incorrect numbers and dimensions that would lead to an unstable structure.
